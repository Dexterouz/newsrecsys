Biography of Mercia Clark

Posted by Dexterous

Date Posted: 2018-10-28 Time: 05:14:24pm

Marcia Clark is an American prosecutor, author and a television correspondent who came into  limelight for putting Robert John Bardo behind bars for the murder of Rebecca Schaeffer  an American actress and also her huge role in the well-publicized trial O.J Simpson who was accuse of murdering his ex-wife and friend Ron Goldman. The O.J Simpson’s trial brought about a challenging and difficult time in her life as it brought so many scrutiny and backlash from the media and general public. Most of the book she has written are entirely based on crime and court cases and some (fictional and non-fictional) are an excerpt from her career as a prosecutor with the challenges she went through.

Bio

Born Marcia Rachel Kleks on the 31st august, 1953 in Alameda, California in the united states of America, she was raised a Jewish by Rozslyn Kleks (mother) and Abraham Kleks (Father), she has a younger brother who is 6 years younger  than Clark. Due to her father’s job working as a chemist in the food and drug administration, the family where occasionally moving from one city to another.

Clark attended Susan E. Wagner high school, graduated from  the University of California with a degree in political science, she went on to earn a degree in law from the Southwestern school of law. She started off her career as a public defender in los Angeles before becoming a prosecutor in 1981, according her, growing up as a child she find interest in reading crime related novel and also being a victim of rape at age 17 this informed her to become a lawyer, little no wonder she has lived up to her dream of becoming a prosecutor. She did excellently well in her job as a prosecutor, winning a number of high profile cases notably the case of Robert John Bardo whom she (Clark) sent to jail over the murder of young actress Rebecca Schaeffer in 1991 and also notwithstanding the widely publicized trial of former NFL player O.J Simpson for the murder of his ex-wife Nicole Brown, Clark was the lead prosecutor in the case along with Christopher Darden but unfortunately they lost the case despite all their effort to prove that Simpson is guilty of the murder charge. The O.J Simpson trial exposed Clark to series of media scrutiny and backlashes from the public due to her “humorless and stiff” personality. After the O.J Simpson trial, Clark resigned from the district attorney office and left legal practices, currently she is a writer and she has written so many book and novel most of which are centered on crime and court cases, some of the books she has written include Guilt by Degree, the competition, Blood defense, snap judgment and notably a book she co-authored with Teresa Carpenter on the Simpson case, without a doubt.

Apart from being a writer, she has appeared on numerous television series and also has been portrayed in television series which are centered on her career as a prosecutor.

Marcia Clark Ex-Husband Gordon

Marcia Clark got married to Gordon Clark in the year 1980. Gordon Clark is a Computer programmer who is a member of the church of scientology in which Marcia is also a member, there union was foster as a result of their membership in the church of scientology, they had 2 children together, however they got separated in the year 1995. Gordon who is 5years younger than Mercia was still an undergraduate at the time of their marriage while Marcia was working, and due to the nature of how things where he couldn’t deal with the fact that he wasn’t working to earn.

During the O.J Simpson’s trial, he (Gordon) argued in court that Clark hasn’t been spending much time with their children and he was seeking to have full custody of their children. Prior to their marriage, Marcia Clark was previously married to Gabriel Horowitz, an Israeli professional backgammon player however they got divorce in 1980 as the marriage only lasted 4 years, they had no children together.

Affair with Christopher Darden

There were many controversies that surrounded the O.J Simpson’s trial one was Marcia Clark purported intimate relationship with fellow prosecutor in the Simpson’s case Christopher Darden, the pair were reported to have been in an intimate relationship during the trial but according to Darden “they were more than friends”, and there were no intimacy between them. With the trial getting stronger and tougher they spent more time working together on the case and at one time in the trial, Darden became a beacon of support to Marcia as she was going through her children custody case with her ex-husband Gordon Clark.
